Here is my project

My partner, Adam O'Brien, and I built a neuromorphic Computer for our senior capstone lab. This project was one of the most mentally taxing I have ever worked on because of all the debugging in the analog circuits. A neuromorphic computer is designed to compute information like the brain. The TLDR of this computer is that it computes organic data (i.e. sound waves, photos, etc.) faster while consuming less power than large-scale neural networks.

Now that the patent has been accepted and our paper is out I can briefly describe what it is. The computer is comprised of 18 double drain-coupled oscillators that are tied together in a way that leverages reservoir computing. This output then goes to a filter circuit that we read as output. In our case of using human voice as an activation phrase, if the correct activation phrase was spoken then a certain LED would light up indicating so.
